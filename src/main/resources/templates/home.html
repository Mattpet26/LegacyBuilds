Chassis

This is how you control your computers environment. It helps regulate temperature to keep your parts cool, and it also can determine how awesome your rig looks! There are different sizes to consider when buying a case; full tower is big, mid-tower is mid sized, and mini is small.

Motherboard

This is like your bodies central nervous system. It is what tells your computer what and how to think, and where to send that information. It is a vital component that you will plug ALL your parts into in some way or another. This will also be the part that allows you to future-proof your system as new technology comes out.

CPU

Known as a computer processing unit. This is like the brain to your computer! This will help you run multiple tasks at once and is vital to gaming, workstations, and any other task.

GPU

Known as a graphics processing unit. This is like the brawn to your computer! This will help you run visual intensive tasks like gaming. It will be the thing that determines if you can play at high settings, or low settings.

RAM

Known as random access memory. This is like your short term memory to your computer. This will help you temporarily store and access active running data and code for your system to use. When gaming it is good to have access to at least 16gb if not 32gb of memory.

HDD / SSD

Known as Solid State Drive or Hard Disk Drive. This is like your long term memory or permanent storage. This is what you will save your data to that you will later access to use again at any time. SSD’s are faster because there are no spinning disks or mechanical parts, where an HDD is mechanical and typically slower to access your data. There are different kinds of SSD’s which may be faster than others. There are kinds that plug directly into your motherboard (fastest); kinds you plug in externally 2.5” drives (fast) and then the HDD typically comes in 3.5” drives (medium speed). These parts will determine how fast your load times are as well.

Power Supply Unit

Known as the power supply unit. This is the heart to your system that pumps the electricity throughout. The bigger your PSU, the more power you have access to, and the more you can plug in. The grades go bronze, silver, gold, and platinum...which determine how reliable over time your power supply will perform and last.

Fans

Fans are pretty straightforward, you need them.

AIO

Known as “All in one” and is meant for water cooling. This is how your CPU stays cool. When your system overheats it can cause anywhere from damage to crashing to lagging. Water cooling is typically more expensive than air cooling but is more effective at regulating temperature.
